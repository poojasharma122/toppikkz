Family Resorts

Best Family Hotels & Kid-Friendly Resorts in Las Vegas

Las Vegas might be known for nightlife and casinos, but there’s plenty here for families, too. From wave pools and aquariums to arcade lounges and spacious suites, these hotels offer experiences that kids and parents can enjoy together. Our Family Time Stays list features the top family-friendly hotels and resorts in Las Vegas—each one picked for comfort, convenience, and kid-approved fun.

Mandalay Bay Resort & Casino

★ ★ ★ ★

Price per night: $159

  • Beach-style pool with wave pool and lazy river

  • Shark Reef Aquarium on-site

  • Family suites available

Mandalay Bay tops the list for family travel. The massive pool complex offers hours of entertainment, while the Shark Reef Aquarium gives kids something unique to explore. Large rooms and direct Strip access keep things easy for parents.

The Mirage Hotel & Casino

★ ★ ★ ★

Price per night: $149

  • Secret Garden and Dolphin Habitat

  • Volcano show every night

  • Central Strip location

The Mirage blends classic Vegas energy with family-friendly touches. Kids love the dolphin habitat and outdoor volcano show, and adults will appreciate the comfortable rooms and proximity to everything on the Strip.

New York-New York Hotel & Casino

★ ★ ★ ★

Price per night: $139

  • Arcade and roller coaster on-site

  • Great casual dining options

  • Fun, themed décor kids enjoy

New York-New York brings a playful tone to family trips. The on-site coaster and arcade offer built-in entertainment, and the themed spaces make every corner feel like an adventure.

View Hotel

Circus Circus Hotel & Casino

★ ★ ★

Price per night: $69

  • Indoor Adventuredome theme park

  • Free daily circus acts

  • Budget-friendly family rooms

Circus Circus is one of Vegas’ most well-known picks for families on a budget. With its in-house amusement park and free circus shows, it’s a solid base for those travelling with younger kids and looking to keep costs down.

View Hotel

Excalibur Hotel & Casino

★ ★ ★

Price per night: $89

  • Castle-themed rooms and décor

  • Fun Dungeon arcade and show theatre

  • Family-style dining options

Excalibur makes a great first Vegas trip for kids. The medieval theme, large arcade, and live shows add a layer of fantasy, while the prices and room sizes keep it family practical.

Hilton Grand Vacations Club on the Strip

★ ★ ★ ★

Price per night: $159

  • Spacious suites with kitchens

  • No casino, quieter environment

  • Great for longer family stays

Hilton Grand Vacations is ideal for families who want a home base rather than a quick stay. The apartment-style suites with kitchens and laundry are perfect for extended trips or younger kids who need downtime between outings.

Tahiti Village Resort

★ ★ ★ ★

Price per night: $129

  • Lazy river and large outdoor pool

  • Condo-style suites with full kitchens

  • Off-Strip location for less noise

Tahiti Village is a top pick for relaxing family stays. It’s away from the Strip noise but still accessible. The big suites, calm setting, and resort-style amenities give families space to settle in and enjoy the trip at their own pace.

The Venetian Resort

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Price per night: $249

  • Spacious suites with sunken living rooms

  • Gondola rides and large shopping/dining area

  • Easy access to attractions

The Venetian is one of the few luxury properties that still works well for families. The suites are large, the atmosphere is lively but not overwhelming, and there’s plenty to do within walking distance—including gondola rides that are a hit with kids.

Desert Rose Resort

★ ★ ★ ★

Price per night: $109

  • Fully equipped kitchens and separate bedrooms

  • No casino for a family-friendly setting

  • Outdoor pool and BBQ area

Desert Rose offers the comforts of home just minutes from the Strip. It’s especially good for families with younger children or those needing more space without the noise of a casino environment.

View Hotel

South Point Hotel, Casino & Spa

★ ★ ★ ★

Price per night: $109

  • On-site bowling alley and movie theatre

  • Arcade and family pool area

  • Free parking and shuttle service

South Point is great for families who want more of a local Vegas feel. With a full bowling alley, arcade, and movie theatre on-site, it’s packed with ways to keep the kids entertained—without breaking the bank.

Booking a Family Stay in Vegas?

Las Vegas has more family-friendly options than you might expect—you just need to know where to look. These hotels are built with space, entertainment, and comfort in mind.

Look for resorts with pools, arcades, or live shows
Consider hotels with kitchens or multiple bedrooms
Stay slightly off-Strip if you want more quiet and space
